Interests

  Decentralized Technology
I have been mesmerized by Bitcoin and other decentralizing protocols since I was lucky enough to stumble into a weird decentralist basement in Vancouver in 2015. I think that the tools and projects being spawned in the disruptive technology sector right now will either liberate humanity, or create more totalizing and inescapable global oppression than we've ever imagined. I'm desperately hoping for the former and have my hat in the ring to help in any way I can.
  Free and Open Source Software
To me, the promise of decentralized technology can only be pursued in tandem with the complex communal incentives that arise around free and open bases of knowledge and code sharing; Richard M. Stallman has long been a hero of mine, which is the reason I rap as msGNU. I believe that the necessary reframing of flows of collaborative value within development communities can resemble familial and tribal networks of support, and we can work in this model towards sating the Tragedy of the Commons.
  Universally Accessible Education
Providing opportunities for all living human beings to access resources, information, and a platform upon which their voices can be heard. Special interest in projects that will accelerate closing the gap between the Global South and Global North, or those that will empower traditionally underserved communities.
  Censorship Resistance
Implementing new models and protocols to structurally "Lock the Web Open" (term borrowed from http://www.decentralizedweb.net/), from Bitcoin for transactions to Tor, VPNs and other privacy-protecting tools, to decentralized data storage solutions.
  Copyright and Patent Reform
Reforming archaic copyright regulations and increasing widespread access to crucial research and information (especially by encouraging use of Creative Commons, GPL, and CopyLeft licenses). Again, Stallman is a hero for me here, as are Lawrence Lessig and all brave and daring remix artists of the modern age.
  Transdisciplinary Creativity
Crafting educational material which translates "Ivory Tower" academia into accessible artistic multimedia (i.e. creating the "Schoolhouse Rock" of critical theory). Getting folks of all ages involved in participatory learning experiences which explore multiple perspectives (h/t Paolo Freiere).
  Human-centric Technology Design
In September 2019, I'm beginning a Master's in Library and Information Science at the University of British Columbia, pursuing participation in the "Designing For People" Specialization. Most of my life's work has pointed in this direction-- I feel that many of the above points are intertwined with necessarily designing our platforms and applications to work for a broad and diverse range of humans, rather than trying to teach all of humanity to think exactly how the product engineers think. I encourage anyone working in this broad transdisciplinary area to reach out to me-- even if I am not the right fit to collaborate with your project, I can make appropriate introductions in my university department.
